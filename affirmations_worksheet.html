Small Group Activity

Mining for Affirmations

Affirmations are more than compliments — they are observations of a client's genuine strengths, values, and efforts. This activity builds the habit of noticing what's already there.

Instructions

Part 1: Think about a current or recent client. In the fields below, identify as many client strengths as you can observe — look for effort, values, resilience, insight, relationships, and past successes.

Part 2: Once you've named your strengths, scroll down. Each strength will appear on the left. Practice converting it into a genuine affirmation — a "you" statement that reflects the strength back to the client in a meaningful way.

Remember: Affirmations center the client and their strengths — not the clinician's reaction to them.
