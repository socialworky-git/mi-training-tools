Mining for Affirmations

Affirmations are more than compliments — they are observations of a client's genuine strengths, values, and efforts. This activity builds the habit of noticing what's already there.

Instructions: Think about a current or recent client. In Part 1, identify as many strengths as you can observe — look for effort, values, resilience, insight, relationships, and past successes. In Part 2, practice converting each one into a genuine affirmation you could say directly to that client.
Affirmations center the client and their strengths — not the clinician's reaction to them.
