Responding to Reluctance

In this activity we explore MI-adherent responses to a client who has concerns about completing a substance use screening. Work with your group to develop each of the six responses below.

Instructions: Read the client statement carefully as a group. Then work together to craft each of the six MI-adherent responses. Use the "Show example" toggle under each item to check your work after you've made your own attempt.
Client Statement
Client "It's a little confusing to me why you're asking about drug use. It doesn't seem like this is relevant to the reason for my visit today. I do want to take care of my health, but the idea of discussing my drug use is making me a bit uncomfortable.

It's not that I don't trust you or the clinic, but it just doesn't feel like this is any of your business. I guess I worry that this information could get out there and that it might cause some sort of legal or social problems for me. I guess it makes some sense that you would need a complete picture of my health and that drugs are part of that. I'm just not sure that this screening is necessary."
